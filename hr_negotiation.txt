FAQ — NEW HR EXECUTIVE GUIDE
============================================================

Q: Is it possible to convince a recruit to accept 10% less static pay?
A: Yes, with these conditions:
   1. Quantify non-monetary benefits in ₹ (ESOP, PTO, parent cover)
   2. Offer written commitment on early review or title upgrade
   3. Candidate's concern must be long-term trajectory, not monthly cash flow
   If candidate has high EMIs, 10% less static pay is nearly impossible to close.

Q: When does a joining bonus work?
A: WORKS: gap is 5–12%, concern is one-time (unvested ESOP, relocation).
   FAILS: concern is recurring monthly affordability (EMIs, rent).

Q: How do I handle a competing offer?
A: 1. Ask "Is their offer the dream job or is ours?" — get them to state their preference
   2. Quantify non-cash benefits in ₹ (PTO days × daily rate, ESOP, insurance value)
   3. Add joining bonus to bridge year-one gap
   4. If they still choose competitor, exit gracefully and keep the door open

Q: What salary gap is realistically closeable?
A: Up to 8%   → easily closeable with total comp reframe
   8–12%      → closeable with title upgrade + written early review
   12–15%     → possible with strong non-monetary package + peer references
   Above 15%  → very difficult; be transparent early
   Above 20%  → almost always declines; graceful exit is best strategy

Q: How do I reduce someone's salary during restructuring?
A: 1. Separate the cut from their performance — it is NOT about them
   2. Use phased reduction over 6–12 months instead of immediate cut
   3. Skills retention allowance as contractual bridge component
   4. Written roadmap showing when band recovers
   5. Performance-linked exception process if they exceed targets

Q: What non-monetary benefits have real ₹ value?
A: - Extra PTO: 7 extra days × (annual salary / 260) = real rupee value
   - Parent health cover: ₹60,000–₹1,20,000/year at zero premium
   - ESOP: intrinsic value at last round valuation, compare vesting cliffs
   - Learning budget: ₹50,000–₹1,00,000/year
   - WFH savings: ₹2,000–₹5,000/month on commute
   - Meal vouchers, fuel allowance: tax-advantaged components
